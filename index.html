The story

"Nobody in my family understood investing. $70k a year was the dream."

My parents were immigrants. They worked themselves into the ground — because that's what they knew. Clock in, clock out, survive. The idea that your money could work while you slept? That conversation never happened at our table.

The richest person I knew growing up made $70,000 a year. That was it. That was the ceiling. We didn't know about compound interest. We didn't know about index funds. We didn't know that time in the market — not picking the right stock — is the only game that actually matters.

I'm 30 now. And I ran the math. I saw what I could have had if someone had sat me down at 22 and shown me a compound interest table. It made me want to cry — not out of regret, but out of urgency. Because I still have time. And so do you.

This isn't a finance newsletter. It's not stock tips or day-trading advice or options strategies. It's the letter that someone should have mailed to both of us a long time ago. From the version of us that figured it out. Written like your future self is reaching back through time, frustrated and loving, saying: just start. It doesn't have to be complicated. You don't have to be smart. You just have to know.

— The founder, Austin TX
